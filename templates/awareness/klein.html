Here Are Some Tips

  • Protect yourself from things that are not suitable for kids. Don't visit those websites or share that stuff. If you see something that bothers you, talk to your parents or an adult you trust.
  • If someone is being mean or talking in a way that makes you feel uncomfortable, it's okay to leave and not respond.
  • If someone is using bad words or being scary online, you can block them, so they can't bother you anymore.
  • Always be careful if someone, especially a stranger, wants to talk about things that make you uncomfortable.
  • If someone tricks you into doing something you shouldn't, tell a trusted adult right away.
  • If you see something online that's really wrong or makes you worried, you should tell someone like the police or a grown-up who can help.
  • Remember to take care of your computer or phone. Keeping them safe is important.

For more information and help, you can visit   Childhelp National Child Abuse Hotline .
